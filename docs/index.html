Concept

Why the feedback loop matters

Learning changes ecological structure, ecological structure changes selection pressures, and selection changes which forms of plasticity persist.

01

Evolution shapes learning capacities.

Selection determines which kinds of adjustment are available, how fast they can respond, and what they cost.

02

Learning reshapes ecological structure.

Behavioral adjustment changes who survives, who interacts, and how local resource and social conditions are organized.

03

Ecological structure reshapes selection gradients.

Once the environment is altered, the fitness landscape changes and different strategies gain or lose advantage.

04

Plasticity closes that loop.

Plasticity links short-run learning to long-run evolution because it changes how organisms track conditions before selection acts on the result.

Unstable Environments

Higher plasticity may be favored because rapid adjustment helps organisms track changing conditions.

Stable Environments

Lower plasticity may be favored because it avoids unnecessary cost and preserves reliable behavior.
